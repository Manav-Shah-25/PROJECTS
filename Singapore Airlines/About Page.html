Know about the
journey of Singapore Airlines

|Our Heritage

Singapore Airlines officially took flight in 1972 following the split of Malaysia-Singapore Airlines. Tracing its roots back to Malayan Airways in 1947, the carrier began strictly as an international airline with a small fleet. Directed by the government to become self-sufficient, it rapidly modernized and expanded its global reach.

The carrier famously introduced the iconic "Singapore Girl" in 1972, cementing a reputation for world-class, luxurious hospitality.
It established itself as a pioneering aviation powerhouse by being the global launch customer for the massive Airbus A380 in 2007 and operating ultra-long-haul routes.

Today, it operates out of its primary hub at Singapore Changi Airport as a premier, Skytrax-awarded five-star carrier.

|To us, travel is more than just being in motion. It’s being in with innovation

ORIGIN AND EARLY YEARS

1947

▼

Malayan Airways Limited was launched. It operated its inaugural flight on May 2—a chartered journey from Singapore to Kuala Lumpur—using an Airspeed Consul aircraft.

1963

▼

Following the formation of the Federation of Malaysia, it was rebranded as Malaysian Airways.

1966

▼

After Singapore's separation from the federation, the airline was reorganized and renamed Malaysia-Singapore Airlines (MSA).

SPLIT AND EXPANSION

1972

▼

Due to differing strategic priorities—Singapore wanted to focus on global expansion while Malaysia preferred domestic routes—MSA was dissolved.
This led to the creation of two distinct entities: Malaysian Airline System (MAS) and Singapore Airlines (SIA).

1977

▼

SIA became the first airline to operate the Boeing 747 in the Asia-Pacific region, beginning services on the lucrative "Kangaroo Route" to London.

1987

▼

The airline launched its iconic and enduring metallic gold and dark blue livery, and the signature "Singapore Girl" donning her
Pierre Balmain-designed sarong kebaya became a globally recognized symbol of the brand

MODERN MILESTONES AND INDUSTRY FIRSTS

1990s

▼

SIA cemented its reputation for a young fleet and elevated service, expanding deep into the American, European, and Asian markets.

2004

▼

It launched the longest non-stop commercial flight in the world at the time, connecting Singapore directly to Newark Liberty International Airport in the United States.

2007

▼

SIA made aviation history by becoming the first airline to fly the massive Airbus A380, revolutionizing premium air travel with features like private Suites.

RESILIENCE AND THE 21ST CENTURY

Pandemic Disruption & Recovery

▼

The airline suffered severe financial setbacks during the COVID-19 pandemic.
However, strong backing from Singapore's state investment fund, Temasek Holdings, allowed the airline to weather the storm.

Current Status

▼

Today, Singapore Airlines has fully bounced back, routinely topping global aviation rankings.
Through its parent operations and subsidiaries like Scoot, it connects over 120 destinations worldwide.
