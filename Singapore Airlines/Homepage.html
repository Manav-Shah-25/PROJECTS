Singapore Airlines (SIA or SQ) is the flag carrier of Singapore with its hub located at Changi Airport. The airline is ranked as a 5-star airline and has been ranked as the world's best airline by Skytrax five times.

Singapore Airlines Group has more than 20 subsidiaries, including numerous airline-related subsidiaries. SIA Engineering Company handles maintenance, repair and overhaul (MRO) business across nine countries with a portfolio of 27 joint ventures including with Boeing and Rolls-Royce. Singapore Airlines Cargo operates SIA's freighter fleet and manages the cargo-hold capacity in SIA's passenger aircraft.

Scoot, a wholly owned subsidiary, operates as a low-cost carrier.

The airline is also notable for highlighting the Singapore Girl as its central figure in the corporate branding segment and not significantly changing its livery throughout its history.

Trending Destinations

| New York City

New York City is the most populous metropolis in the US, comprised of five unique boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island. As a global hub for business and culture, it is home to world-renowned landmarks like Times Square, Central Park, and the Statue of Liberty.

| Bangkok

Bangkok, the capital of Thailand, is a bustling megacity of over 11 million people, famed for its vibrant street life and ornate shrines. Situated along the Chao Phraya River, it beautifully blends centuries-old culture with modern skyscrapers and mega-malls. The city is universally celebrated for its sizzling street food, bustling floating markets, and iconic landmarks like the Grand Palace.

| London

London is the vibrant capital and largest city of the United Kingdom. Straddling the River Thames, it is a major global hub for business, finance, and culture. The city boasts world-famous landmarks like the Tower Bridge, Buckingham Palace, and the British Museum. London is home to the world's oldest underground railway, affectionately known as "The Tube".

| Tokyo

Tokyo is Japan's bustling capital and one of the world's most populous metropolitan areas. It seamlessly blends ultra-modern, neon-lit skyscrapers with serene historic temples and traditional gardens. Renowned for its unparalleled culinary scene, it boasts everything from vibrant street food to world-class Michelin-starred dining. The metropolis is a global hub for technology, fashion, and pop culture, while still remaining remarkably clean and safe.

| Paris

Paris is the capital and largest city of France, globally renowned as a hub for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. Known as the "City of Light," its iconic cityscape is defined by the winding River Seine, Haussmannian boulevards, and landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and Notre-Dame cathedral.

| Mumbai

Mumbai is the bustling capital of Maharashtra and India’s financial and commercial powerhouse. Often called the "City of Dreams," it serves as the beating heart of the nation's economy and is home to the booming Bollywood film industry. From iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India to its vibrant, multicultural nightlife, the city is a seamless blend of rich history and modern energy.

| Melbourne

Melbourne, the capital of Victoria, is Australia's cultural and sporting capital. Renowned for its iconic laneway culture, exceptional coffee, and vibrant street art, it frequently ranks among the world's most livable cities. The city is also home to the largest urban tram network in the world.

| Dubai

Dubai is the most populous city in the United Arab Emirates, globally celebrated for its luxury shopping, ultra-modern architecture, and vibrant nightlife. Rising rapidly from the Arabian Desert, the city is famous for record-breaking landmarks like the towering 828 m Burj Khalifa and the sprawling Dubai Mall.
